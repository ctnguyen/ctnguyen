Laminar flow in a pipe

Navier-Stokes 2D stationary

The laminar flow of fuid in a pipe is described by the Navier-Stokes equation. The equations system relying the velocity fields and pressure is given by :

\[ (\wp)\hspace{0.25cm}\left\{ \begin{array}{rl} (\textbf{u}.\triangledown) \textbf{u} -\nu \bigtriangleup \textbf{u} + \triangledown p & =0 \\ \triangledown \textbf{u} & =0 \\ \textbf{u}_\Gamma &=g \end{array}\right. \]

This nonlinear system after being linearized is discretized by the Newton scheme becomes:

\[ (N)\hspace{0.25cm}\left\{ \begin{array}{rl} -\bigtriangleup \textbf{u}^{n+1} + \frac{1}{\nu}[( \textbf{u}^n. \triangledown) \textbf{u}^{n+1}+ (\textbf{u}^{n+1} .\triangledown) \textbf{u}^n] + \triangledown p^{n+1} &=\frac{1}{\nu}(\textbf{u}^n.\triangledown)\textbf{u}^n \\ \text{div}\textbf{u}^{n+1} &=0 \\ \textbf{u}^{n+1}_{|\Gamma_D} &=g \end{array}\right. \]

This project is the depth study of mathematical modeling

  • Study of the existence and uniqueness of the continuous solution
  • Linearization and discretization of the equation in time and space
  • Advanced finite element method (Taylor-Hood)
  • Numerical integration by quadrature formulas
  • Advanced C++ programming (template, virtual function)
  • Programming GUI with Qt
